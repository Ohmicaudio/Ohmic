# Ohmic Business Operations Model

## Purpose

Define the day-to-day human operating model for building, running, and scaling
Ohmic as a business, not just as a codebase.

Use the status meanings from:

- [OHMIC_PLAN_STATUS_DEFINITIONS_2026-03-14.md](/mnt/b/ohmic/docs/roadmap/OHMIC_PLAN_STATUS_DEFINITIONS_2026-03-14.md)

## Core Rule

The business should run on repeatable lanes, not founder memory.

Every meaningful business task should land in one of these lanes:

- product and build
- acquisition and content
- conversion and monetization
- customer success and community
- operations and administration
- hardware program and R and D

## Daily Operating Lanes

### 1. Product And Build

Purpose:

- ship real improvements
- keep product trust increasing
- keep queues and claims honest

Daily jobs:

- close or advance one real product task
- verify current live surfaces still work
- record next-step tasks instead of leaving them in chat

Outputs:

- shipped work
- clean queue state
- visible product movement

### 2. Acquisition And Content

Purpose:

- attract people who do not know Ohmic yet

Daily jobs:

- publish or prepare one search, social, or educational asset
- improve one existing landing or tool surface
- capture one reusable proof artifact: screenshot, clip, chart, or build note

Outputs:

- traffic surfaces
- reusable content inventory
- trust signals

### 3. Conversion And Monetization

Purpose:

- turn attention into email capture, trials, upgrades, or paid support

Daily jobs:

- review offer clarity
- check upgrade paths and CTAs
- inspect where people would get confused or fall out of the funnel

Outputs:

- clearer offers
- better upgrade prompts
- stronger pay path

### 4. Customer Success And Community

Purpose:

- turn users into return users and advocates

Daily jobs:

- answer open support or community questions
- harvest recurring pain points
- convert repeated questions into docs, UI, or automation improvements

Outputs:

- stronger trust
- lower support drag
- product learning from real users

### 5. Operations And Administration

Purpose:

- keep the business legible and survivable

Daily jobs:

- maintain planning truth
- maintain task and claims truth
- track key metrics
- keep repo, docs, offers, and channels aligned

Outputs:

- lower confusion
- cleaner operating state
- fewer hidden fires

### 6. Hardware Program And R And D

Purpose:

- advance hardware without letting it eat the software business

Daily jobs:

- sort research into intake packets
- reduce unknowns in requirements and interfaces
- keep hardware plans concrete enough to critique

Outputs:

- clearer module packets
- lower hardware risk
- stronger readiness for later schematic work

## Minimum Daily Scoreboard

Every operating day should be understandable through a simple scoreboard:

- `shipped`: what real thing moved
- `published`: what outward-facing thing was made
- `captured`: what lead, insight, asset, or proof was saved
- `answered`: what customer/community issue was closed
- `clarified`: what operational confusion was removed

If a day produces none of those, the system is probably busy but not advancing.

## Weekly Rhythm

### Monday

- set priority
- choose the 1-3 business-critical outcomes for the week
- confirm which work is execution and which is still review

### Tuesday To Thursday

- execution-heavy days
- ship product
- publish content
- improve conversion
- answer users

### Friday

- review metrics
- clean queues and claims
- summarize what actually moved
- set up the next week so Monday starts clean

## Human Task Classes

Use these owner classes until the team is bigger:

- `founder / director`
  - direction, approval, hard tradeoffs, voice, partner-level decisions
- `execution agent`
  - docs, code, systems, planning packets, queue truth, implementation
- `content and growth lane`
  - SEO pages, social clips, content backlog, lead magnets, publishing cadence
- `customer and community lane`
  - support, feedback, help requests, moderation, trust surfaces
- `hardware R and D lane`
  - requirements, interfaces, research, block diagrams, risks

One person may temporarily cover multiple lanes, but the lanes should remain
distinct in the docs.

## Expansion Rule

Do not hire or delegate into a blur.

Expand by lane:

1. stabilize the lane
2. define its scoreboard
3. define repeatable tasks
4. then delegate or automate

## Operating Systems To Build

### Content System

- idea capture
- page or clip production
- publish queue
- performance review
- refresh cycle

### Offer System

- free offer
- paid offer
- hardware-linked offer
- CTA placement
- upgrade path

### Support System

- intake
- classify
- answer
- turn repeated pain into fixes or docs

### Hardware Intake System

- collect research
- sort into module packets
- promote into requirements and interfaces

## Red Flags

The business operating system is drifting if:

- too many decisions live only in chat
- tasks are active but unclaimed
- content gets published without a funnel role
- product work ships without any outward proof or narrative
- support pain repeats without entering docs or product queues
- hardware planning jumps to schematic work without requirements

## Immediate Interpretation For Ohmic

Right now the business should be treated as:

- software-first revenue engine
- content-led acquisition machine
- support and community trust builder
- hardware incubator running in parallel, not the whole business

That is the operating truth this model is designed to support.
