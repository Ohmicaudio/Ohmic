# Ohmic Content And Channel Cadence

## Objective

Define the first repeatable content system for Ohmic so publishing supports
acquisition, trust, and conversion instead of becoming random output.

## Core Rule

Every channel needs a job.

If a content item does not serve a clear job in the funnel, it is noise.

## Channel Set

### 1. Search And SEO

Job:

- capture intent from people already looking for a tool, answer, or workflow

Primary surfaces:

- static tool pages
- static learning pages
- platform and hardware pages
- suite hubs that route into deeper workflows

Current source material:

- `design/*`
- `measurement/*`
- `learn/*`
- `hardware/*`
- `platform/*`
- `community/*`

Artifact types:

- tool landing pages
- tightly scoped educational pages
- comparison pages
- troubleshooting pages
- “how to” pages

Success signal:

- traffic to relevant entry pages
- movement from static pages into suite hubs, account creation, or app entry

### 2. Social Proof And Short-Form

Job:

- make the product feel real, active, and worth following

Primary surfaces:

- short clips
- screenshots
- before and after visuals
- quick explainers
- “what this tool solves” micro-demos

Artifact types:

- 15 to 60 second clips
- one-screen walkthroughs
- annotated charts
- single-problem, single-solution posts

Success signal:

- saved clips
- profile follows
- clicks into tools or suite pages

### 3. Build Log And Authority Content

Job:

- build trust with serious users by showing real thinking, real process, and
  real hardware/program progress

Primary surfaces:

- hardware planning progress
- design and measurement methodology posts
- lessons learned
- behind-the-scenes system-building notes

Artifact types:

- build logs
- technical notes
- module planning snapshots
- “why we designed it this way” posts

Success signal:

- trust and credibility
- repeat visits
- stronger brand authority among serious users

## Content Families

### Family A: Problem-Solver Content

Purpose:

- answer a specific search or user pain quickly

Examples:

- speaker box calculator entry pages
- impedance measurement pages
- diagnostics pages

Best channels:

- SEO
- short-form follow-up clips

### Family B: Workflow-Upgrader Content

Purpose:

- show that the paid path saves time, reduces confusion, or unlocks better
  outcomes

Examples:

- advanced design workflow walkthroughs
- measurement workflow continuity
- project save/share/export value stories

Best channels:

- suite hubs
- app landing surfaces
- short-form demo clips

### Family C: Authority Content

Purpose:

- prove that Ohmic understands the field deeply enough to deserve trust

Examples:

- hardware planning notes
- design tradeoff breakdowns
- measurement interpretation walkthroughs

Best channels:

- build logs
- technical posts
- deeper short-form explainers

## Weekly Cadence

### Monday

- choose the 3 highest-leverage items for the week
- assign one item to each channel lane:
  - SEO
  - short-form
  - authority/build-log

### Tuesday

- publish or improve one SEO page
- capture one proof artifact from current product or graphics work

### Wednesday

- publish one short-form piece based on:
  - a tool
  - a visual
  - a measurement or design insight

### Thursday

- publish one build-log or authority post
- tie it to hardware planning, workflow learning, or product reasoning

### Friday

- review what shipped
- note which items created:
  - clicks
  - questions
  - interest
  - useful follow-up ideas
- stage next week’s content candidates

## First Publish Recommendations

### First SEO push

Use the strongest existing static entry surfaces:

- speaker box calculator
- speaker port calculator
- speaker impedance measurement
- audio system diagnostics

Why:

- these map cleanly to obvious search intent
- they connect directly to the design and measurement thesis

### First short-form push

Use artifacts from active work already happening:

- graphics before and after replacements
- one fast walk-through of a design or measurement tool
- one visual explaining what Ohmic actually is

Why:

- the proof already exists
- it avoids making fake polished content disconnected from the real product

### First authority push

Use the hardware program and operating-system work:

- why software pays for hardware
- how the hardware program is being structured
- what modules exist and why

Why:

- it creates narrative continuity
- it makes the long-term vision feel grounded instead of hand-wavy

## Conversion Tie-In

Each channel should point somewhere:

- SEO -> tool or suite entry
- short-form -> tool, suite, or app entry
- authority -> platform understanding, waitlist, or deeper interest path

No channel should exist as pure vanity output.

## Operating Rule

One weekly content set should ideally produce:

- one SEO improvement
- one short-form proof asset
- one authority/build-log asset

That is enough to build consistency without turning the business into a media
factory too early.

## Recommendation

Run this cadence lightly but consistently. The point is not volume. The point is
that every week should leave behind:

- better search capture
- one more real proof artifact
- one more trust-building authority signal
