# Ohmic Business Operations Year One Roadmap Proposal

## Objective

Build a business that can repeatedly acquire attention, convert the right
customers, support them well, and expand into hardware without collapsing under
founder load.

## Operating Thesis

The business should scale in this order:

1. trust
2. attention
3. conversion
4. retention
5. expansion
6. hardware scale-up

That is the operational complement to the product thesis that software pays for
hardware.

## Current Reality

The business has:

- a growing software platform
- a meaningful static-content and SEO surface
- a hardware ambition becoming a structured hardware program
- no fully locked operating system yet for day-to-day business execution

This proposal fills that gap.

## Week 1 Proposal

### Goal

Stand up the minimum business operating system.

### Proposed work

- finalize offer ladder
- finalize founder and execution lane rhythm
- define channel jobs for SEO, social, and build logs
- define support and community response loop
- set a tiny scoreboard that can be reviewed weekly

### Expected result

- the business becomes legible
- daily work stops feeling like random context switching

## Month 1 Proposal

### Goal

Operate like a small but real company instead of a project with momentum.

### Proposed work

- run a weekly content cadence
- run a weekly review cadence
- improve upgrade prompts across app and static surfaces
- set a simple lead and inquiry log
- convert repeated support questions into product, docs, or content improvements

### Operating outputs

- one visible content pipeline
- one visible inquiry pipeline
- one visible review ritual

## Quarter 1 Proposal

### Goal

Turn the business into a repeatable loop.

### Proposed work

- make the offer ladder and conversion path measurable
- formalize community/help and paid support boundaries
- publish consistent technical proof and product demos
- define what counts as marketing content, educational content, and product
  proof
- turn hardware planning into outward authority content without overpromising

### Operating result

- clearer customer journey
- stronger confidence in what content drives what outcome
- lower chaos in weekly planning

## Six Month Proposal

### Goal

Expand from founder-led hustle into a system that can support specialized lanes.

### Proposed lane expansion

- `content and growth`
- `customer and community`
- `operations and admin`
- `hardware R and D`

### Proposed delegation rule

Each lane gets handed off only after:

- its repeating tasks are clear
- its scoreboard exists
- its standards are written down

### Operating result

- less founder bottleneck
- better consistency across publishing, support, and planning

## Twelve Month Proposal

### Goal

Operate as a credible software business with a serious hardware program, not as
an overextended idea factory.

### Proposed business outcomes

- repeatable acquisition engine
- clear free-to-paid ladder
- growing customer proof and community trust
- stable weekly and monthly operating cadence
- enough operational maturity to support hardware pilots responsibly

### Proposed founder state

The founder should be spending less time on:

- reconstructing context
- chasing task truth
- re-deciding the same priorities

and more time on:

- approval decisions
- partner-level direction
- high-leverage product and market moves

## Daily Human Task Model

### Every Day

- ship or advance one product or content asset
- publish, schedule, or capture one outward-facing proof artifact
- respond to open support or feedback signals
- review the scoreboard
- log the next real task instead of trusting memory

### Every Week

- choose the top 1-3 business outcomes
- review traffic, conversions, support signals, and shipped work
- cut or defer low-value noise
- queue the next week's execution packet

### Every Month

- review what channels are working
- review what offers are converting
- review what support pain is repeating
- review whether hardware work is still paced correctly relative to software
  revenue

## Proposed Scoreboards

### Acquisition

- content shipped
- pages improved
- proof artifacts created
- top traffic surfaces

### Conversion

- leads captured
- upgrade clicks
- activated premium users
- support-to-paid conversions

### Retention And Trust

- support requests answered
- repeated pain points closed
- community/help participation
- product or doc fixes created from feedback

### Operational Health

- planning docs current
- queue truthful
- claims truthful
- weekly review completed

## Risks

- trying to market too many things at once
- overbuilding operations before revenue exists
- treating social activity as progress without conversion learning
- letting hardware excitement steal all execution from software and business ops
- keeping too much business truth in the founder's head

## Review Questions

- should the business initially optimize for services and support revenue,
  premium software revenue, or a mixed model
- which public voice should lead the brand:
  - technical teacher
  - builder and experimenter
  - product-system operator
  - all three in rotation
- how much public energy should go into hardware vision versus current software
  utility

- how the company runs
- what gets measured
