# Ohmic Business Scoreboard And Weekly Review Ritual

Date: 2026-03-14

## Purpose

Turn the business operations model into a weekly ritual that is small enough to
run consistently and concrete enough to change what gets worked on next.

This is not a finance deck or a vanity dashboard. It is a founder-usable review
surface for:

- what moved
- what did not move
- where trust or demand is showing up
- what should become queued work next

## Core Rule

If the scoreboard cannot be updated in under 15 minutes, it is too heavy.

If the weekly review does not produce at least one explicit keep, cut, or queue
decision, it is theater.

## Scoreboard Categories

Use five categories only:

1. shipped
2. published
3. captured
4. answered
5. clarified

These already match the operating model and keep the review focused on visible
movement instead of vague effort.

## Minimum Metrics List

### Shipped

- product or system changes shipped this week
- bugs or blockers closed this week
- one sentence on what moved from idea into working reality

### Published

- docs, pages, clips, or public proof shipped this week
- number of meaningful updates, not draft fragments
- note which piece is most likely to help trust or acquisition

### Captured

- leads captured
- email replies or interest signals worth following up
- reusable proof assets saved
- repeated insight worth promoting into docs, product, or offer copy

### Answered

- customer or community questions answered
- average first-response time if it is easy to observe
- count of issues fully closed instead of partially acknowledged

### Clarified

- internal confusion removed
- decisions made that reduce drift
- queue, docs, or process fixes that make later execution easier

## Scoreboard Format

Use one row per category:

| Category | This week | Signal | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Shipped | count + top item | up / flat / down | what actually moved |
| Published | count + top item | up / flat / down | what is now public or usable |
| Captured | count + top signal | up / flat / down | what demand or proof was saved |
| Answered | count + response note | up / flat / down | what trust work got closed |
| Clarified | count + top fix | up / flat / down | what confusion was removed |

`Signal` can stay qualitative until volume is high enough to justify tighter
measurement.

## Weekly Review Ritual

Run this once per week in 20 to 30 minutes.

### Before the review

- update the five scoreboard rows
- gather any simple counts already visible from email, queue, docs, or product work
- pull the top 3 useful notes from the week

### During the review

1. Confirm the top business-critical outcome for the week that just ended.
2. Read the scoreboard row by row.
3. Name the strongest positive signal.
4. Name the most important stall or miss.
5. Decide what to keep doing.
6. Decide what to stop, defer, or ignore.
7. Convert the next real work into queued tasks.

### After the review

- write a 5-line weekly summary
- queue the follow-on tasks immediately
- set the 1 to 3 business-critical outcomes for the next week

## Weekly Review Checklist

- did something real ship
- did something outward-facing publish
- did we capture real demand, proof, or contact
- did we answer people fast enough to build trust
- did we remove operational confusion
- what repeated pain showed up at least twice
- what is the one business action that matters most next week

## Decision-To-Queue Rule

Every review decision must end in exactly one of these states:

- `keep` and no task needed
- `watch` and record it in next week's review notes
- `queue` and create a request the same day

If the answer is `queue`, the request must include:

- the concrete outcome
- the owner lane
- the proof that will show it worked
- the earliest reasonable review date

No review conclusion should live only in notes or memory.

## Lightweight Founder Version

If time is bad, run the compressed version:

1. fill the five rows
2. write one win
3. write one problem
4. queue one next task

That is enough to keep the rhythm alive.

## Failure Signs

This ritual is failing if:

- counts are filled in but no decisions change
- the same pain point appears three weeks in a row
- support or demand signals are noticed but never queued
- the scoreboard becomes a list of activity instead of outcomes

## Recommendation

Start with a plain Markdown or note-based scoreboard, not a tool-heavy dashboard.

Consistency is more valuable right now than precision. Once the weekly ritual is
stable, the metrics can get sharper without collapsing the habit.
