# Ohmic Customer Response And Feedback Loop

Date: 2026-03-14

## Purpose

Create the minimum usable system for handling customer questions, community
signals, and repeated pain without losing them between chat, docs, and product
work.

The goal is trust and learning first. Speed matters, but only if the answer or
follow-up actually closes the loop.

## Core Rule

Every inbound signal must end up in one of four states:

- answered
- waiting on user
- queued for fix or doc work
- intentionally declined

Nothing should stay as an orphaned message.

## Intake Paths

Start with a small set of intake paths:

### 1. Direct support inbox

Use for:

- product questions
- account or payment questions
- bug reports
- pre-purchase questions

This should be treated as the primary support lane.

### 2. Site contact or form submissions

Use for:

- general contact
- partnership or collaborator interest
- lead capture with a question attached

These should be triaged into support, sales, or partner follow-up quickly.

### 3. Community and social replies

Use for:

- public questions
- confusion triggered by content
- repeated comments that show unclear messaging

These are not second-class signals. They often show where trust is weak or
docs are missing.

### 4. Internal founder notes

Use for:

- remembered customer pain from calls or DMs
- repeated objections
- language that clearly resonates

These notes only count if they are promoted into the queue or the weekly
review. Memory alone is not a system.

## Intake Triage Fields

Every item should be tagged with a few simple fields:

- source
- date
- person or handle if known
- category
- urgency
- owner lane
- next action

Use these categories:

- support
- pre-purchase
- product bug
- docs gap
- feature request
- partner or business
- community trust signal

## Response Expectations

Keep the expectations honest and lightweight.

### First response target

- direct support inbox: within 1 business day
- site contact: within 1 business day
- public/community question: same day if easy, otherwise within 1 business day

### Closure target

- simple question: within 1 to 2 business days
- bug or product issue: acknowledge within 1 business day, then move into queue
  with status visible to the team
- feature request: acknowledge and classify, do not promise shipment

### Response standard

Every response should try to do one of these:

- answer directly
- ask the one missing question needed to unblock
- point to the canonical doc or product path
- explain what will happen next and when

## Owner Model

Use lane ownership before role ownership.

### Founder / director

- partner or business outreach
- hard customer exceptions
- voice-sensitive responses
- final tradeoff calls on promises or refunds

### Customer and community lane

- first-pass support triage
- reply drafting
- follow-up tracking
- repeated-pain collection

### Execution agent

- bug reproduction
- docs fixes
- queue creation
- turning repeated pain into concrete implementation packets

## Repeated-Pain Promotion Path

When the same pain appears twice, promote it out of chat and into the system.

### If the pain is confusion

- queue a docs or content update
- improve copy, screenshots, examples, or setup steps

### If the pain is friction

- queue a product or workflow fix
- capture what exact step stalled the person

### If the pain is objection or hesitation

- queue offer or messaging work
- record the phrase the person used, not just the abstract topic

## Promotion Rule

Escalate to queued work when any of these is true:

- the same pain appears twice in one week
- one issue blocks purchase, setup, or trust for multiple people
- the answer requires repeating the same explanation manually
- a public question exposes a docs or product gap that others will likely hit

## Feedback Outputs

The loop should feed four downstream surfaces:

- docs updates
- product fixes
- offer or copy refinement
- weekly review notes

If a signal does not land in one of those places, it is probably being lost.

## Weekly Review Inputs

Bring these into the weekly review:

- total inbound items
- total answered
- repeated pain points
- top unresolved issue
- one trust-building win

This is enough to learn without building a fake support dashboard.

## Minimal Workflow

1. capture the inbound item
2. classify it
3. respond or acknowledge
4. decide whether it needs queue promotion
5. log the outcome in the weekly review if it taught something useful

## Failure Signs

This loop is failing if:

- questions are answered but never captured
- repeated pain keeps showing up with no doc or product change
- first responses are fast but closures never happen
- public replies and private inboxes tell different stories

## Recommendation

Start with one visible inbox, one lightweight tracker, and one weekly review
surface.

The system should be good at catching repeated pain and turning it into better
docs, clearer product behavior, and more trustworthy responses. Scale can come
later.
