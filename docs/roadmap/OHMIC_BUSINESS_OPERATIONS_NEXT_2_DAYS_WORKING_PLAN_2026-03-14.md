# Ohmic Business Operations Next 2 Days Working Plan

## Objective

Build the first usable business operating layer so Ohmic stops relying on loose
mental tracking for offers, channels, customer response, and founder-level next
steps.

## Why Now

- the product and content machine is now real enough to support business ops
- hardware planning has a home, so operations can stop blurring hardware and
  software decisions together
- without an operating layer, marketing, monetization, and support will remain
  reactive and inconsistent

## Scope In

- define the human operating model
- define the first daily and weekly business cadence
- define the first offer ladder in operating terms
- define the first acquisition and customer-response loops
- define the scoreboards and review points

## Scope Out

- full CRM setup
- payroll or hiring process design
- legal entity restructuring
- formal bookkeeping system migration
- ad-buying programs

## Owners

- `founder / director`: approve operating model, approve offer ladder, decide
  channel voice and tradeoffs
- `execution agent`: build the docs, queue the follow-on work, define the
  repeatable systems
- `content / growth lane`: execute publishing and capture loops after approval
- `customer / community lane`: execute response and feedback loops after
  approval

## Day 0 To Day 1

### 1. Lock the business operating model

Why:

- the business needs lane discipline just like the product work does

How:

- publish the business operations model
- define daily lanes and weekly rhythm
- define the scoreboard

Output:

- business operations model doc

Exit criteria:

- lane structure exists
- daily and weekly rhythms are explicit

### 2. Define the first business offer structure

Why:

- product surfaces are useless commercially if the offer is unclear

How:

- name the first offer ladder:
  - `free`
  - `pro`
  - `hardware-linked`
- define what each level is for
- define what should drive upgrade pressure

Output:

- offer ladder section inside the business roadmap proposal

Exit criteria:

- each tier has a job, not just a name
- first paid entry point is identifiable

### 3. Define the first acquisition loop

Why:

- marketing needs a repeatable machine, not random posting

How:

- define the first 3 source lanes:
  - search and SEO
  - social proof and short-form
  - build-log and authority content
- define what content each lane should publish

Output:

- channel and content cadence section

Exit criteria:

- every channel has a job
- content production has a repeatable weekly cadence

## Day 1 To Day 2

### 4. Define the first customer and community loop

Why:

- support pain and user questions are future product and content assets

How:

- define intake paths
- define response expectations
- define how repeated questions convert into docs, content, or product tasks

Output:

- customer-success loop section

Exit criteria:

- support questions have a visible path
- repeated pain has a route into the product/content system

### 5. Define the founder daily and weekly operating checklist

Why:

- founder attention is the scarcest resource

How:

- define what must be checked daily
- define what must be reviewed weekly
- separate approval work from execution work

Output:

- founder operating cadence section

Exit criteria:

- daily founder checklist exists
- weekly founder review exists

### 6. Queue the first follow-on operational tasks

Why:

- planning without queued execution creates another dead packet

How:

- create explicit follow-on tasks for:
  - offer page copy and positioning
  - content calendar setup
  - customer response process
  - scoreboard and metrics review

Output:

- ready tasks in the shared queue

Exit criteria:

- at least 3 concrete business-ops follow-on tasks exist

## Deliverables Due Inside 48 Hours

- business operations model
- business operations roadmap proposal
- offer ladder in operational form
- acquisition loop definition
- customer/community loop definition
- founder cadence definition
- follow-on business tasks queued

## Approval Questions

- is the first paid motion better framed as `pro workflow`, `support access`, or
  `hardware-adjacent premium`
- which audience should be served first operationally:
  - builders
  - installers
  - competitors
  - measurement-focused power users
- should founder social output lean more technical, more founder-journey, or a
  blended model

## Exit State

If this 2-day business plan succeeds, Ohmic should end the window with:

- a visible human operating model
- a named offer ladder
- a real acquisition loop
- a real customer-response loop
- a cleaner founder operating rhythm
